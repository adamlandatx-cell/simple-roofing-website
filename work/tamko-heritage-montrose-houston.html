What the Homeowner Wanted

A spring storm with straight-line winds made the decision for this Montrose homeowner. They woke up to shingles in the yard and exposed underlayment visible from the street. The roof was already 22 years old — worn but holding on. The wind event pushed it past the point of reasonable repair.

They didn't want to file an insurance claim. They wanted a straightforward replacement with a contractor they chose.

Our Solution

The wind damage was concentrated on the west-facing slope. But the inspection revealed the rest of the roof wasn't far behind. A targeted repair would have been temporary.

We recommended Tamko Heritage in Rustic Black. The scope included:

  • Complete tear-off (22 years of shingles plus the original layer underneath — it had been a layover)
  • Decking repairs in wind-damaged section
  • Fascia replacement along the west eave
  • Full synthetic underlayment throughout
  • Tamko Heritage shingles with 130 MPH wind warranty
  • Ridge vent installation (the home had no ridge ventilation previously)
