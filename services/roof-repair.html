Common Roof Repairs We Handle

Leak Repair

Leaks often originate far from where water appears inside. We trace the source, repair the penetration point, and fix any water-damaged decking.

Missing or Damaged Shingles

High winds, fallen branches, or age can leave shingles cracked, curled, or missing entirely. We match your existing shingles as closely as possible.

Flashing Repair

Flashing around chimneys, vents, skylights, and roof edges is a common failure point. We reseal or replace flashing to stop leaks at their source.

Pipe Boot Replacement

The rubber boots around plumbing vents crack and fail after 10-15 years. A $200 boot replacement can prevent thousands in water damage.

Ridge Cap Repair

Ridge caps take the most wind abuse. We replace blown-off or damaged ridge caps to restore your roof's seal at the peak.

Fascia & Soffit Repair

Water damage often extends beyond shingles to the fascia boards and soffit panels at your roof's edge.

Repair or Replace? How We Help You Decide

We won't push replacement if repair makes sense. Here's our honest framework:

Repair Usually Makes Sense When:

  • Your roof is under 15 years old
  • Damage is isolated to one area
  • No signs of widespread granule loss or curling
  • Decking underneath is solid

Replacement Usually Makes Sense When:

  • Roof is 20+ years old
  • Multiple leak points or widespread damage
  • Shingles are curling, cracking, or losing granules throughout
  • Decking has soft spots in multiple areas

Roof Repair FAQs

How much does a roof repair cost?

Minor repairs typically run $150-400. Larger repairs can range $500-1,500. We provide exact pricing after inspection.

Can you match my existing shingles?

We try our best. Shingle colors fade over time, so a perfect match on an older roof is rare. We select the closest available match.

How fast can you come out?

For non-emergencies, we typically schedule inspections within 2-3 business days. For active leaks, we prioritize same-day or next-day response.
